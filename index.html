About Me

Noah Gans is a passionate GIS Engineer with a robust foundation in computational methods for solving urban and transit-related problems, a passion ignited during his college thesis. With a Bachelor of Arts in Computer Science & Sociology (Cum Laude) from Bowdoin College, Noah has a strong academic background complemented by significant real-world experience. His professional journey includes impactful roles such as Demand Planning Data Analyst at Backroads, where he led revenue-improving initiatives through data analytics, and an internship at NASA & Jet Propulsion Laboratory, focusing on High Altitude Balloon Technologies. Noah's academic projects, like his award-winning Computational Geospatial Honors Project, showcase his ability to synthesize computational findings with academic literature to address societal issues. His programming skills span Python, C++, Java, among others, with proficiency in data analysis and visualization tools like ArcGIS and QGIS. Noah's interests extend beyond his professional and academic pursuits, including hobbies like playing cards, pottery, and skiing, reflecting a well-rounded personality with a commitment to both personal and professional growth
